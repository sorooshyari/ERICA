This gallery compares ERICA visualizations against figures from the clustering stability literature. All analyses use ERICA v0.1.4 with B=200 MC iterations, 80/20 train-test split. Datasets include VDX breast cancer (3-gene subset), Gaussian sigma sweeps, and 2D shape datasets. Click any figure to enlarge.

Assignment Similarity Matrices

Sample × sample matrices showing assignment profile similarity, inspired by Monti et al. (2003) consensus matrices. Computed as the inner product of L1-normalized CLAM rows; rows/columns reordered by hierarchical clustering.

ASM-01
VDX 3-gene — K-Means
Real breast cancer gene expression data. Three panels: K=2, 3, 4.
Plot typeAssignment similarity matrix (consensus heatmap variant)
LiteratureMonti, Tamayo, Mesirov & Golub (2003) Consensus Clustering, Machine Learning 52:91-118 (Fig. 2). Also: ConsensusClusterPlus (Wilkerson & Hayes 2010, Bioinformatics 26(12):1572-1573). Note: our computation (CLAM inner product) approximates but differs from the Monti consensus matrix.
Datasetvdx_3gene — 344 samples, 3 features. No ground-truth K; panels at K=2, 3, 4
MethodK-Means | panels at true_k−1, true_k, true_k+1 (fallback K=3)
Iterations200 | Train/test split: 80/20
X / Y axesSamples (reordered by hierarchical clustering of 1−similarity)
ColorAssignment profile similarity: 0=never co-assigned (white), 1=always (dark blue)
Computationclam_normed = L1_normalize(CLAM); similarity = clam_normed @ clam_normed.T
NoteNOT the Monti consensus matrix. Diagonal < 1.0 for uncertain samples
Reorderingscipy.cluster.hierarchy.linkage(1−similarity, method='average') → leaves_list
ColormapBlues (matplotlib), vmin=0.0, vmax=1.0
ERICA callget_results()['clam_matrices'][(k, 'kmeans')]
