This gallery explores which visualizations of the CLAM matrix and ERICA's replicability metrics are most useful for understanding internal cluster structure. All analyses use ERICA v0.1.4 with B=200 MC iterations, 80/20 train-test split. Primary methods: K-Means and Agglomerative Clustering (Ward and Single linkage). Figures generated with matplotlib; viridis and Okabe-Ito colorblind-accessible palettes.

*HDBSCAN integration is under active development and not yet fully validated. HDBSCAN figures are included for exploratory purposes only.

1. CLAM Matrix Heatmaps

The Cluster Labels Alignment Matrix (CLAM) records, for each sample, how many times it was assigned to each cluster across B Monte Carlo iterations. Rows sorted by primary cluster (argmax), then by assignment confidence within each cluster.

Sorted CLAM — VDX, K-Means, K=3
344 samples, 3 genes (ESR1, ERBB2, AURKA) from Parmigiani et al. Sorted by primary cluster then confidence. Viridis colormap, assignment counts 0 to B.
clam_matrices[(3, 'kmeans')]
Figure 1b
Unsorted CLAM — VDX, K-Means, K=3
Same data, original sample order. Block structure obscured without sorting.
Figure 1c
CLAM across K=2, 3, 4 — VDX, K-Means
Progression of sorted CLAM heatmaps as K increases. Shows how existing clusters subdivide and where off-diagonal assignments appear.
Figure 1d
HDBSCAN CLAM — VDX, modal K=2 *experimental
CLAM from iterations where HDBSCAN discovered K=2. Noise points reassigned to nearest centroid. Shape: (344, 2). Note: HDBSCAN integration is under validation.

8. Gaussian Mixture Sigma Study

4 isotropic Gaussian centers in 50 dimensions, 100 samples per center. Centers separated by 9.49 (Euclidean) in the first 10 dimensions. Standard deviation σ swept from 0.01 to 10.0, yielding radius/spacing ratios from 0.01 (trivially separated) to 7.45 (indistinguishable). K=2..6 tested with all four methods.
